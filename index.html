Mahesh Reddy Tapeti

I am a undergrad robotics researcher driven by the goal of creating intelligent systems that bridge the gap between human perception and machine efficiency. My journey into robotics began with a simple question: how can we make machines safer and more effective in handling labor-intensive, hazardous tasks while addressing their limitations in perception and adaptability? This curiosity has shaped my academic and professional pursuits.

At the Robotics Research Center, IIIT Hyderabad, I had the opportunity to work under the guidance of Prof. Nagamanikandan Govindan, exploring imitation learning to develop systems capable of operating effectively in real-world environments. I'm currently working on guidance of Prof. Madhava Krishna With a strong interest in combining physics-based principles and learning-based approaches, my work is focused on pushing the boundaries of what robots can achieve in complex environments. Apart from this I'm involved in a project with Matthew Lisondra, PhD UofT, focussing on solving high dimensional data issues in manipulation.
